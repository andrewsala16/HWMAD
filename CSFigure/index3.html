Dennis Ritchie
The Invention of Unix While Ritchie was working at Bell Labs in the later 1960s, he and Ken Thompson incented Unix. The goal was to create a time-sharing computer system for the office. The first version of Unix was on a PDP-7 minicomputer. They worked on making it adaptable to other computers. Soon the patent department at Bell Labs began using the system for their daily work.
The Invention of 'C' Language A successor to the programming language B, C was made specifcally for running on Unix. C is made for machine instructions and is still being used for assembly language. It was designed to be compiled to provide low-level access to memory. main() { printf("hello, world\n"); }
Awards Ritchie has won a variety of prestigious awards for his work on Unix and C. In 1983, he received the Turing Award for Unix. In 1990, he received the IEEE Richard W. Hamming Medal for Unix and C. In 1997 he was made Fellow of the Computer History Musemeum. In 1999, he recieved the National Medal of Technology from Bill CLinton.
